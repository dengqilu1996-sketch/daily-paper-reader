Abstract 24 

25 

Globin digest (GD), an acidic protease hydrolysate of hemoglobin, has been recognized for its 26 

anti-obesity and glucose-modulating effects; however, its direct anabolic potential in skeletal 27 

muscle remains uncharacterized. We evaluated the effects of GD and its constituent peptides 28 

on muscle hypertrophy and motor function using zebrafish, mice, and C2C12 myoblasts. Adult 29 

zebrafish administered GD (400 mg/kg BW/d) for 1 week showed significantly increased 30 

swimming distance ( p < 0.05). Similarly, oral administration of GD (1 g/kg BW/d) to C57BL/6J 31 

mice for 4 weeks enhanced grip strength and rotarod performance, accompanied by a 1.5-fold 32 

increase in myofiber diameter and upregulation of fast-twitch Myh1 (1.9-fold) and Myh2 (1.8-33 

fold) mRNA levels. In vitro , GD dose-dependently (1–100 μg/mL) stimulated C2C12 34 

differentiation and MyHC accumulation. Notably, GD did not merely serve as a nutritional 35 

nitrogen source; instead, it functioned as a signaling modulator via a specific "relay-like" 36 

peptide orchestration. Among six identified sequences, Peptides 3 (WTQR) and 5 (WGK) 37 

primarily initiated early-stage commitment by upregulating MyoD and Myf5 , whereas Peptides 38 

2 (VVYP) and 6 (FES) accelerated mid-stage maturation. This stage-specific synergy achieved 39 

robust myotube hypertrophy that exceeded the efficacy of individual components. These 40 

findings demonstrate that GD promotes skeletal muscle hypertrophy and motor function 41 

through direct myogenic signaling, establishing a novel foundation for precision sports nutrition 42 

to optimize muscle maintenance and physical performance. 43

1. INTRODUCTION 76 

Optimizing skeletal muscle mass and function is a critical priority for both elite athletes 77 

aiming for peak performance and the general population seeking to maintain metabolic health [1– 

78 

> 3]

. To maximize muscle protein synthesis, current nutritional guidelines recommend a high daily 79 

protein intake of 1.6 to 2.2 g/kg body weight for athletes [4] . However, achieving these targets 80 

through conventional dietary sources while maintaining high bioavailability remains a81 

significant practical challenge. Athletes frequently encounter a "quantity versus quality" trade-82 

off; increasing total protein intake to meet physiological demands can lead to gastrointestinal 83 

distress and suboptimal absorption efficiency, ultimately hindering consistent nutritional 84 

management [5–7] .85 

To overcome these physiological bottlenecks, nutritional strategies must shift from merely 86 

supplying bulk building blocks to efficiently stimulating the skeletal muscle regenerative 87 

program. Myogenesis is a highly orchestrated process regulated by myogenic regulatory factors 88 

(MRFs). During early differentiation, Myf5 and MyoD proteins drive the commitment and 89 

proliferation of myogenic progenitor cells, followed by the upregulation of muscle-specific 90 

myosin heavy chain ( MyHC ) genes ( Myh1 , Myh2 ), which leads to the functional maturation 91 

and hypertrophy of fast-twitch (Type II) fibers [8–10] . Because Type II fibers are critically 92 

responsible for explosive power and athletic performance [11,12] , compounds that directly trigger 93 

this Myf5- or MyoD-mediated pathway are highly sought after. In this context, bioactive short-94 

chain peptides have emerged as a potent alternative to intact proteins [13–16] . They are rapidly 95 

internalized into cells via independent, high-capacity peptide transporters such as PepT1 [17] and 96 

PHT1 [18] and function as powerful signaling ligands capable of inducing MRF expression at 97 

significantly lower doses [19,20] .98 

Food-derived bioactive oligopeptides have gained considerable attention for their diverse 99 

biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, anti-fatigue, and metabolic regulatory 100 

effects [21–23] . Among these, globin digest (GD), obtained through the acidic protease treatment 101 

of porcine hemoglobin, is approved as a functional food in Japan and China [24] . GD provides 102 

potent metabolic benefits, including improvements in postprandial hyperlipidemia and 103 

hyperglycemia [25,26] . Our recent studies demonstrated that GD exerts anti-obesity effects by 104 

upregulating uncoupling protein 1 (UCP1)-related pathways and enhancing systemic energy 105 

expenditure. Furthermore, we previously demonstrated that GD upregulates the expression of 106 

Ucp2 and Ucp3 genes specifically within skeletal muscle, where they are thought to enhance 107 

fatty acid oxidation and energy expenditure. Crucially, we observed that GD-treated in vivo 108 

models exhibited a marked reduction in visceral fat without a corresponding decrease in total 109 

body weight, a phenomenon accompanied by preliminary evidence of upregulated myogenic 110 

biomarkers [27] . These findings led to the hypothesis that the anti-obesity effects of GD are 111 

complemented by a concomitant increase in muscle mass, pointing to a beneficial modulation 112 

of body composition through the adipo-muscular axis. Despite these promising systemic 113 

observations, the direct cellular mechanisms by which GD modulates the myogenic program 114 

and determines muscle fiber-type composition remain uncharacterized. Furthermore, the 115 

specific peptide sequences within the GD mixture responsible for these direct anabolic effects 116 

have not been identified. Therefore, determining whether GD optimizes the intramuscular 117 

microenvironment to support Type II fiber hypertrophy represents a novel paradigm in sports 118 

nutrition. 119

In the present study, we employed an integrated in vivo and in vitro approach to evaluate the 120 

effects of GD on skeletal muscle differentiation and motor function. First, we assessed motor 121 

function improvements in zebrafish, followed by an evaluation of exercise performance and the 122 

expression of key myogenic genes ( Myf5, MyoD, Myh1, Myh2, Myh3, and Myh7 ) in a mouse 123 

model. Furthermore, to elucidate the underlying molecular mechanisms, we investigated the 124 

direct effects of GD on myogenesis and MyHC assembly using C2C12 myoblasts. Additionally, 125 

we identified six specific peptide sequences within GD and characterized their individual roles 126 

in promoting myogenic differentiation. These findings establish the multifaceted nutritional 127 

value of GD and provide a foundation for novel nutritional strategies to support efficient muscle 128 

maintenance and performance enhancement in athletes. 129

through the National Bio-Resource Project of the MEXT, Japan. The cells were cultured in a 152 

37 °C, 5% CO 2 incubator using a growth medium composed of Dulbecco’s Modified Eagle’s 153

The differentiation medium was refreshed every other day. GD (1, 10 or 100 μg/mL) and 158 

individual peptides (1 μg/mL) were added at the onset of differentiation. 159

2.5. Zebrafish experiment 169 

For the oral administration of GD to adult zebrafish, zebrafish food containing 10% (w/w) 170 

GD was prepared using gluten as a carrier material, as previously described [30] . Briefly, 3-171 

month-old female zebrafish were randomly assigned to either the control or GD group (five fish 172 

per 2-L tank). The control group was fed a normal diet (gluten granules; 2 mg/fish/day) 173 

throughout the 1-week experimental period. The GD group was fed GD-containing gluten 174 

granules (2 mg/fish/day), corresponding to a dose of 400 mg/kg body weight/day. At the end 175 

of the feeding experiment, body weights were measured, followed by the electrically stimulated 176 

swimming test. For this test, individual zebrafish were placed in a rectangular tank (17.5 cm × 177 

14.5cm) and allowed to acclimate for at least 5 min. Once the fish ceased spontaneous 178 

movement and became completely motionless, a 5-V electrical pulse was applied for 0.1 s from 179 

electrodes positioned at both ends of the tank to induce swimming. The experimental arena was 180 

filmed from directly above at 30 frames per second using a digital high-definition (HD) video 181 

camera (HC-V495M; Panasonic, Osaka, Japan). The stimulus-evoked swimming speed, 182 

acceleration, and distance traveled were subsequently analyzed using ToxTrac software 183 

(version 1.1) [31] .184

Toyama, Japan), allowing for the simultaneous measurement of all four limbs. Motor 193

3. RESULTS 255 

3.1. GD promotes muscle hypertrophy and locomotor ability in zebrafish 256 

To evaluate the in vivo effects of GD on skeletal muscle performance, we first utilized a 257 

zebrafish model. Young zebrafish (3 mpf) were orally administered GD (400 mg/kg body 258 

weight) for one week. Prior to administration, the standard length (SL) and body weight (BW) 259 

of the control group were 2.0 ± 0.1 cm and 0.11 ± 0.02 g, respectively, while those of the GD 260 

group were 1.9 ± 0.1 cm and 0.09 ± 0.01 g. After the 1-week feeding trial, the SL increased to 261 

2.1 ± 0.1 cm in the control group and 2.0 ± 0.2 cm in the GD group, representing a 5% increase 262 

in both groups. Concurrently, the BW increased to 0.13 ± 0.02 g in the control group (a 13% 263 

increase) and 0.12 ± 0.01 g in the GD group (a 19% increase). Although the GD-treated group 264 

exhibited a greater percentage of weight gain, no statistically significant differences were 265 

observed in either SL or BW between the groups ( Figure S1 ). Following the treatment, an 266 

electrically stimulated forced swimming test was conducted to assess functional output. As 267 

shown in Figure 1 , the GD-treated group demonstrated robust improvements across multiple 268 

kinematic parameters. Specifically, the average acceleration during the escape response was 269 

significantly ( p < 0.05) increased by 2.9-fold compared with the control group ( Figure 1A ). 270 

Similarly, the maximum burst acceleration was markedly elevated by 3.8-fold ( Figure 1B , p <271 

0.05). This pronounced enhancement indicates that GD administration specifically amplifies 272 

the explosive motor power of zebrafish. Consistent with the elevated acceleration, the average 273 

swimming speed was also significantly higher, showing a 90% increase in the GD-treated group 274 

(Figure 1C , p < 0.05). To investigate whether this functional enhancement was associated with 275 

structural changes in the musculature, we performed a histological analysis using hematoxylin 276 

and eosin (HE) staining of the trunk skeletal muscle (Figure 1D) . Representative images 277 

revealed that zebrafish treated with GD exhibited visibly larger skeletal muscle fibers than those 278 

in the control group. Morphometric analysis showed that the muscle fiber diameter in the GD-279 

treated group was 1.39-fold thicker than that of the control group ( Figure 1E , p < 0.01). 280

with the control ( Figure S4B ). 361

4. DISCUSSION 415 

In the present study, we demonstrated that globin digest (GD) and its specific constituent 416 

peptides directly promote skeletal muscle hypertrophy and enhance physical performance. Our 417 

results revealed that GD administration significantly increases muscle fiber diameter and 418 

enhances explosive motor function across different species. Furthermore, the identification of 419 

a stage-specific peptide relay provides a novel mechanistic explanation for the potent anabolic 420 

effects of the natural GD mixture. 421 

422 

4.1. Functional expansion of GD: The adipo-muscular axis 423 

Our previous research established that GD exerts anti-obesity effects by upregulating UCP1-424 

mediated thermogenic pathways and suppressing adipocyte hypertrophy in zebrafish and mouse 425 

models [27] . Our previous findings highlighted that the metabolic impact of GD is mediated not 426 

only by UCP1 in adipose tissue but also by the systemic activation of other UCP family 427 

members, including Ucp2 and Ucp3 in skeletal muscle. Since UCP3 is primarily expressed in 428

skeletal muscle and is known to protect mitochondria from oxidative stress during fatty acid 429 

oxidation while increasing energy expenditure within myocytes [39–41] , the potentiation of 430 

myogenic differentiation observed in this study may synergistically enhance these UCP-431 

mediated metabolic benefits. 432 

Given the fundamental role of the adipose-muscle crosstalk in systemic metabolic 433 

homeostasis [42–44] , these findings provide a molecular basis for the hypothesis that GD acts as a 434 

multi-target nutrient. Notably, GD-treated obese models in our previous study exhibited a 435 

significant reduction in visceral adipose tissue without a corresponding loss in total body 436 

weight [27] . At that time, we hypothesized that this maintenance of body mass was due to a 437 

concomitant increase in muscle volume. Crucially, the present study confirmed that GD 438 

administration induces hypertrophy of skeletal muscle fibers (Figure 2D) and upregulates the 439 

expression of myogenic markers, including Myh1 (Figure 3A) and Myh2 (Figure 3B) in vivo. 440 

By demonstrating that GD directly acts on muscle progenitors to facilitate their maturation, the 441 

current C2C12 results provide the crucial mechanistic link (Figure 5), confirming that the 442 

observed muscle preservation is driven by the direct enhancement of myogenesis. 443 

The metabolic benefits of GD are not confined to adipose tissue but extend to the functional 444 

improvement of skeletal muscle, potentially reflecting the integrated metabolic crosstalk 445 

between these tissues. Furthermore, GD administration was previously shown to upregulate 446 

Prkaa2 (AMPK) and Bdnf [27] , both of which are critical regulators of lipid catabolism and the 447 

maintenance of muscle fiber integrity [45,46] . This dual functionality aligns with recent trends in 448 

molecular nutrition, where bioactive molecules such as GLP-1 receptor agonists [47] and 449 

Angiotensin-(1-7) [48] are identified as significant modulators of both lipid metabolism and 450 

muscle regenerative capacity. The ability of GD to facilitate myogenesis while simultaneously 451 

ameliorating visceral adiposity suggests a promising nutritional strategy targeting the adipo-452 

muscular axis, providing a comprehensive intervention for both the clinical management of 453 

sarcopenic obesity [49] and the enhancement of athletic power-to-weight ratios to maintain 454 

physical independence across the lifespan. 455

4.3. Molecular basis for the myogenic initiation by WTQR and WGK 522 

Within this multistage model, the early initiation of the myogenic program by Peptide 3 523 

(WTQR) and Peptide 5 (WGK) serves as the critical first step. To understand the molecular 524 

basis of this robust induction, we focused on their structural characteristics. To date, only a few 525 

specific short-chain peptides have been recognized as direct signaling ligands. For instance, the 526 

collagen-derived dipeptides prolyl-hydroxyproline (Pro-Hyp) [50] and hydroxyprolyl-glycine 527 

(Hyp-Gly) [51] have been reported to promote myotube hypertrophy by modulating the 528 

Akt/mTOR/Foxo3a signaling pathway. While these collagen dipeptides primarily act to 529 

stimulate protein synthesis and suppress degradation in existing myotubes, our findings reveal 530 

that the GD-derived peptides, WTQR and WGK, exert a more profound upstream effect. By 531 

directly upregulating myogenic regulatory factors such as MyoD and Myf5 , these specific GD 532 

constituents actively drive the initial commitment and differentiation of myocytes. 533 

The unique ability of WTQR and WGK to trigger this upstream signaling is likely 534 

attributable to their specific structural characteristics, specifically the arrangement of aromatic 535 

(Trp) and basic (Arg, Lys) amino acids. This structure-activity relationship suggests that the 536 

specific amino acid sequence, rather than total nitrogen content, is the primary driver of the 537 

observed effects. The potency of these sequences may be attributed to the inclusion of 538

results demonstrated that GD administration led to evident myofiber hypertrophy in mice, 561 

which directly correlated with enhanced whole-body motor performance, including increased 562 

grip strength and rotarod endurance. Furthermore, the significant improvement in stimulus-563 

evoked burst swimming speed and acceleration in zebrafish underscores the practical impact of 564 

GD on explosive power. In our in vivo analysis, GD significantly upregulated the expression of 565

4.5. Limitations and future perspectives 592 

While the multi-model approach employed in this study provides robust evidence for the 593 

pro-myogenic effects of GD, several limitations must be considered. First, although the 594 

identification of specific peptides that induce MyoD-mediated MyHC expression serves as a 595 

powerful strategy for muscle enhancement, human clinical trials are indispensable to confirm 596 

the clinical efficacy of the proposed peptide orchestration strategy, particularly regarding 597 

optimal dosage and timing for athletes. Furthermore, it should be noted that this study did not 598 

include a direct comparative analysis between GD and conventional intact proteins regarding 599 

gastrointestinal tolerance or net muscle protein synthesis efficiency at equivalent doses. While 600 

the low-dose efficacy of GD observed in our models is encouraging, future research is required 601 

to confirm whether GD intake effectively mitigates digestive distress while maintaining peak 602 

performance compared to standard high-protein diets. 603

5. Conclusion 605 

In conclusion, this study establishes GD and its constituent peptides as potent signaling 606 

modulators that transcend the traditional role of protein as a mere building block. By 607 

demonstrating that a specific “peptide relay” directly activates the myogenic program and 608 

modulates the adipo-muscular axis, GD offers a targeted strategy for “Precision Sports 609 

Nutrition” to optimize performance and a novel approach to combat sarcopenic obesity. These 610 

findings provide a robust foundation for unlocking the full physiological potential of the human 611 

body to support lifelong functional vitality. 612
