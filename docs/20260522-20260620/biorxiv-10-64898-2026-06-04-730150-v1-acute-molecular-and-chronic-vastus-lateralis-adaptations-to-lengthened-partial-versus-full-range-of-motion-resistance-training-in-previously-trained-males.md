## 摘要
本研究探讨了下肢加长部分幅度（LP）与全幅度（FULL）抗阻训练对训练有素男性股外侧肌（VL）的急性运动后信号传导、长期肥大及细胞适应的影响。八名男性（22±1岁，训练经验5.6±1.4年）完成了一项交叉研究，在LP和FULL腿部伸展训练前后0、3和24小时采集VL活检样本，用于转录组学和合成代谢信号分析（实验1）。另外16名男性（26±5岁；训练经验8.0±4.9年）采用受试者内设计，进行了为期8周、每周两次的下肢干预（实验2）。在三种练习（腿举、腿屈伸和俯卧腿弯举）中，一条腿被分配至FULL训练，对侧腿进行LP训练。干预前后的结局指标包括：通过五个等距MRI横向切片测得的VL肌横截面积（mCSA）总和，以及大腿中部VL活检结果。作为次要结局，还评估了实验2 MRI扫描中的其他髋部和大腿肌肉。所有结局的‘条件×时间’交互作用采用线性混合效应模型进行评估。在实验1中，两种条件在VL转录组和合成代谢（mTORC1和Hippo）信号传导方面产生了相似的时间依赖性变化，但方案间交互作用极小。在实验2中，VL总和mCSA随时间显著增加（平均变化：9.3 cm²，95% CI [6.8, 11.8]，P<0.001），但没有明确证据表明不同方案间存在差异变化（LP-FULL变化：-1.4 cm²，95% CI [-6.1, 3.8]，P=0.640）。此外，在I型肌纤维CSA（P=0.476）、II型肌纤维CSA（P=0.350）、I型肌纤维肌核数（P=0.813）、II型肌纤维肌核数（P=0.589）、I型和II型卫星细胞数量（P分别为0.102和0.797）或总RNA含量（P=0.537）方面均未发现显著交互作用。尽管这些以VL为中心的发现无效，但实验2的次要分析提供了一些证据，表明LP训练后整个腘绳肌的肥大程度高于FULL训练（LP-FULL变化：3.9 cm²，95% CI [-0.2, 7.9]，P=0.058）。总之，在训练有素的男性中，8周的LP和FULL抗阻训练在VL中广泛引起了相似的急性和慢性反应，但次要的腘绳肌发现表明，不同的反应可能取决于抗阻训练计划中包含的练习。

## Abstract
This study examined how lower-body lengthened partial (LP) versus full range of motion (FULL) resistance training affects acute post-exercise signaling, chronic hypertrophy, and cellular adaptations of the vastus lateralis (VL) muscle in resistance-trained men. Eight males (22{+/-}1 years old, 5.6{+/-}1.4 years training) completed a crossover study whereby VL biopsies were collected pre-exercise and 0, 3, and 24 hours following LP and FULL leg extension bouts for transcriptomic and anabolic signaling analyses (Experiment 1). Another 16 males (26{+/-}5 years old; 8.0{+/-}4.9 years training) completed an 8-week, twice-weekly lower-body intervention using a within-subject design (Experiment 2). One leg was assigned to FULL and the contralateral leg to LP training across three exercises (leg press, leg extension, and lying leg curl). Pre- and post-intervention outcomes included VL muscle cross-sectional area (mCSA) summed across five equidistant MRI-derived transverse slices and mid-thigh VL biopsy outcomes. As a secondary outcome, other hip and thigh muscles from Experiment 2 MRI scans were assessed. Condition x Time interactions for all outcomes were assessed using linear mixed-effects models. In Experiment 1, both conditions produced similar time-dependent changes in the VL transcriptome and anabolic (mTORC1 and Hippo) signaling, but minimal between-protocol interactions. In Experiment 2, VL summed mCSA significantly increased over time (mean change: 9.3 cm{superscript 2}, 95% CI [6.8, 11.8], P<0.001), but there was no clear evidence of differential change between protocols (LP-FULL change: -1.4 cm{superscript 2}, 95% CI [-6.1, 3.8], P=0.640). Additionally, no significant interactions existed for type I fiber CSA (P=0.476), type II fiber CSA (P=0.350), type I fiber myonuclei (P=0.813), type II fiber myonuclei (P=0.589), type I and II satellite cell number (P=0.102 and P=0.797, respectively), or total RNA content (P=0.537). Despite these null VL-centric findings, secondary Experiment 2 analyses provided some evidence that whole hamstring hypertrophy was greater following LP versus FULL (LP-FULL change: 3.9 cm{superscript 2}, 95% CI [-0.2, 7.9], P=0.058). In conclusion, 8 weeks of LP and FULL resistance training broadly elicit similar acute and chronic VL responses in previously trained men, though secondary hamstring findings suggest that differential responses may depend on exercises included in the resistance training program.
