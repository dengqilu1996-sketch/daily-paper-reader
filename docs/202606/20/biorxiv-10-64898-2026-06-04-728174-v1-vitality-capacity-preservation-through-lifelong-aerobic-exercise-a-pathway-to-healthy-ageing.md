## 摘要
背景 健康老龄化与病理性老化的区分引出了活力容量（VC）的概念，可理解为身体的生理储备。个体的VC可通过分布在三个领域的12项生物标志物进行估计：免疫与应激反应、能量与代谢以及神经肌肉功能。终身身体活动可能有助于维持活力容量。这项横断面研究旨在探究终身有氧身体活动与VC之间的关系。

方法 对20名终身活跃和19名不活跃的年龄>55岁健康成年人的VC进行评估，采用跨越三个VC领域的12项生物标志物。计算各领域特异性z分数并取平均值以获得整体VC评分。进行主成分分析并提取载荷以估计领域权重，并进行多重相关分析以识别生物标志物、领域和VC评分之间的关联。

结果 终身活跃参与者的VC较高（+0.2 z分数单位，p = 0.006），且与年龄相关（r = -0.53，p < 0.001）。神经肌肉领域对VC变异的贡献最大，免疫与应激反应领域在活跃组中较高（+0.4 z分数单位，p = 0.001），而能量/代谢领域在女性参与者中较高（+0.5 z分数单位，p.adj = 0.006）。

结论 终身有氧身体活动与老年人较高的VC相关，特别是在免疫与应激反应领域。这些发现突显了身体活动在维持生理储备中的作用，并加强了终身有氧身体活动作为健康老龄化驱动因素的相关性。

## Abstract
BackgroundThe distinction between healthy and pathological ageing has led to the concept of vitality capacity (VC), which can be understood as the bodys physiological reserve. An individuals VC can be estimated using 12 biomarkers spread across 3 domains: immune and stress response, energy and metabolism and neuromuscular function. Vitality capacity may be preserved by lifelong physical activity. This cross-sectional study aimed to examine the relationship between lifelong aerobic physical activity and VC.

MethodsVC of 20 lifelong active and 19 inactive healthy adults aged >55 years was assessed using 12 biomarkers across the three VC domains. Domain-specific z-scores were calculated and averaged to derive a global VC score. Principal component analysis was performed and loadings extracted to estimate domains weight, and multiple correlations were conducted to identify associations among biomarkers, domains and VC scores.

ResultsVC was higher in lifelong active participants (+0.2 z-score units, p = 0.006) and correlated with age (r = -0.53, p < 0.001). Neuromuscular domain contributed most to VC variability, and the immune and stress response domain was higher in the active group (+0.4 z-score units, p = 0.001) as energy/metabolism among female participants (+0.5 z-score units, p.adj = 0.006).

ConclusionLifelong aerobic physical activity is associated with higher VC in older adults, particularly within the immune and stress response domain. These findings highlight the role of physical activity in preserving the physiological reserve and reinforce the relevance of lifelong aerobic physical activity as a driver of healthy ageing.
