## 摘要
由于任何感觉输入中都存在不确定性，我们会经历知觉偏差，这些偏差常常污染感觉运动转换。知觉学习或适应如何防止这些偏差，是知觉与运动控制推理理论中的一个开放性问题。我们通过一个经典的低级知觉偏差例子——运动知觉中的孔径问题及其对平滑追踪的影响——重新审视了这一争论。在视觉运动追踪范式中，参与者追踪向左、向右倾斜或竖直运动的光栅斑块。正如预期，初始追踪方向偏向倾斜方向。当参与者在数天的训练中反复追踪单个倾斜光栅时，这种初始追踪误差保持不变。相比之下，训练后误差校正对于训练过的光栅方向变得更快，但对于未经训练的镜像对称方向则没有变化。这种动力学变化可由延迟的补偿性追踪反应的出现来解释，这种反应在训练竖直光栅（通常会引发无偏追踪的刺激）后最能揭示。这种校正性眼动对形状和运动特征均具有选择性。它滞后追踪起始约40毫秒，表明它是由有偏差的运动指令本身触发，而非由视觉再传入触发。这些结果支持了低级感觉计算（在短时间内无法被修改）与感觉运动转换内部模型（可被快速更新以补偿不可消除但可预测的感觉驱动知觉偏差）之间的分离。

## Abstract
Because of the uncertainties present in the any sensory inflows, we experienced perceptual biases that often contaminate sensorimotor transformations. How these biases can be prevented through perceptual learning or adaptation is an open question in inference theories of perception and motor control. We revisited this debate using a classic example of low-level perceptual bias, the aperture problem in motion perception and its consequences for smooth pursuit. In a visuomotor tracking paradigm, participants pursued left-, right-tilted, or upright Gabor patches moving horizontally. As expected, initial pursuit direction was biased toward the oblique directions. This initial pursuit error remained unchanged when participants repeatedly tracked a single tilted Gabor during training sessions conducted over several days. By contrast, post-training error correction became faster for the trained Gabor orientation, but not for its untrained, mirror-symmetric counterpart. This change in dynamics was explained by the emergence of a delayed compensatory pursuit response, best revealed after training with an upright Gabor, a stimulus that would normally elicit unbiased tracking. This corrective eye movement was selective for both shape and motion features. It lagged by [~]40 msec after pursuit onset suggesting that it was triggered by the biased motor command itself rather than by visual reafference. These results support a dissociation between low-level sensory computations, which cannot be modified over short timescales, and internal models of sensorimotor transformation, which can be rapidly updated to compensate for irreducible but predictable sensory-driven perceptual biases.
