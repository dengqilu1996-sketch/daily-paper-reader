## 摘要
内在机制与反馈控制在诸如站立和行走等直立任务中协同工作以稳定身体。然而，表征稳定性的集总参数在不同任务间是否变化以及如何变化尚不清楚。为回答这一问题，我们应用了一个稳定模型，该模型将延迟的质心位置和速度信息与未受扰动的站立和行走中的当前地面反作用力（已校正重力影响）相关联，以估计并比较不同任务间的稳定性。15名健康年轻参与者（21±4岁，63±9千克，1.70±0.10米，13名女性，2名男性）参与了本研究，他们以1.25米/秒的速度行走5分钟，并执行了3种不同的站立任务：正常站立、单腿站立和跨步姿势，每次1分钟，重复5次。收集了全身运动学和地面反作用力数据，用于拟合稳定模型并估计有效延迟和集总增益。结果显示，行走和站立的模型拟合均良好，平均R²值高于0.69。行走中的有效延迟显著长于站立。虽然集总位置增益和速度增益在不同任务和方向上存在显著差异，但集总位置增益大多高于临界刚度，集总速度增益均处于欠阻尼水平，唯一例外是行走中前后方向的集总位置增益，略低于临界刚度。所有任务中集总位置增益与速度增益之比始终接近人体固有频率（sqrt(g/l)），这与外推质心概念所预测的一致。我们的发现表明，稳定性是在任务层面组织的，所有稳定机制的集总效应使得不同任务间位置与速度贡献的权重保持一致，从而有效调节质心运动，使其遵循类似摆动的轨迹。

## Abstract
Intrinsic mechanisms and feedback control work together to stabilize the body in upright tasks, such as standing and walking. Yet if, and how lumped parameters characterizing stabilization change across tasks is unclear. To answer this question, we applied a stabilization model, relating delayed center of mass position and velocity information to current ground reaction forces (corrected for effects of gravity) in unperturbed standing and walking, to estimate and compare the stabilization between tasks. 15 healthy young participants (21+/- 4 yrs, 63 +/- 9 kg, 1.70 +/-0.10 m, 13 females, 2 males) participated in this study, they walked at 1.25 m/s for 5 minutes, and performed 3 different standing tasks: normal standing, unipedal standing, and step posture for 1 minute, repeated 5 times. Whole-body kinematics and ground reaction forces were collected and used to fit the stabilization model and estimate the effective delay and lumped gains. Results showed good model fits for both walking and standing with mean R2 values higher than 0.69. The effective delay was significantly longer in walking than in standing. While the lumped position and velocity gains varied significantly across tasks and directions, the lumped position gains were mostly above critical stiffness and lumped velocity gains all at under-damped level, with an only exception that lumped position gains in the AP direction in walking, which were slightly below critical stiffness. The ratio of lumped position to velocity gains in all tasks remained consistently close to human body's eigenfrequency (sqrt(g/l)) as predicted by the extrapolated center of mass concept. Our findings suggest that stabilization is organized at the task level, with lumped effects of all stabilizing mechanisms acting to preserve a consistent weighting between position and velocity contributions across tasks, effectively regulating the CoM motion to follow a pendulum-like trajectory.
