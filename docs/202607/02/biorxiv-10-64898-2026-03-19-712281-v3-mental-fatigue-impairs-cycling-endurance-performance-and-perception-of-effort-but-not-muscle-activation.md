## 摘要
精神疲劳由长时间参与认知要求高的任务所诱发，并损害耐力表现。这种表现下降背后的神经心理生理机制仍不清楚，有观点认为精神疲劳可能干扰运动指令，进而影响肌肉激活。我们旨在通过一项重复交叉设计研究来检验这一假设，该研究中18名参与者完成了两个实验阶段，包括在80%峰值功率输出下的力竭骑行测试。每次骑行任务前进行1小时的长时间Stroop任务（Stroop条件）或中性对照任务（对照条件）。使用以“完全不疲劳”和“极度疲劳”为锚点的视觉模拟量表评估精神疲劳。在骑行过程中定期记录努力感知和来自右腿十块下肢肌肉的表面肌电图。与对照条件相比，Stroop条件下的精神疲劳更高（p = .002）。Stroop条件下的耐力骑行时间短于对照条件（分别为887 ± 284秒 vs. 998 ± 379秒；-111 ± 160秒，p = .009）。在任何肌肉上，Stroop与对照条件之间的肌电图参数均未观察到显著差异（p > .05）。从骑行任务开始时，Stroop条件下的努力感知就更高（p = .006），并且Stroop条件下努力感知的增加速率显著高于对照条件（p = .031）。我们的发现不支持精神疲劳改变运动控制或增加中枢运动指令的假设，因为未检测到肌肉激活的变化。相反，我们的结果强化了这样一种观点：长时间的认知参与主要通过增加努力感知来损害耐力表现。未来的研究应考虑将表面肌电图与更敏感的神经生理技术相结合，以探究精神疲劳下动态全身任务中运动驱动的潜在微妙变化。

## Abstract
Mental fatigue is induced by prolonged engagement in cognitively demanding tasks and impairs endurance performance. The neuropsychophysiological mechanisms underlying this decreased performance remain unclear, with suggestion that mental fatigue may disrupt motor command and consequently muscle activation. We aimed to test this hypothesis in a repeated cross-over design study in which 18 participants completed two experimental sessions involving a time-to-task failure cycling test at 80% of peak power output. Each cycling task was preceded by 1h of a prolonged Stroop task (Stroop condition) or a neutral control task (Control condition). Mental fatigue was assessed using a visual analog scale anchored with "not fatigued at all" and "extremely fatigued". Perception of effort and surface electromyography from ten lower-limb muscles of the right leg were recorded at regular intervals during cycling. Mental fatigue was higher in the Stroop compared to the Control condition (p = .002). Endurance cycling time was shorter in the Stroop than in the Control condition (887 {+/-} 284 s vs. 998 {+/-} 379 s, respectively; -111 {+/-} 160 s, p = .009). No significant differences in electromyography parameters were observed between Stroop and Control conditions, for any muscle (p > .05). Perception of effort was higher in the Stroop condition from the onset of the cycling task (p = .006), and the rate of increase in perception of effort was significantly higher in the Stroop than Control condition (p = .031). Our findings do not support the hypothesis that mental fatigue alters motor control or increases central motor command, as no changes in muscle activation were detected. Conversely, our results reinforce the notion that prolonged cognitive engagement impairs endurance performance primarily through an increased perception of effort. Future research should consider combining surface electromyography with more sensitive neurophysiological techniques to investigate potential subtle changes in motor drive during dynamic, whole-body tasks under mental fatigue.
