> Abstract. 72 Mental fatigue is induced by prolonged engagement in cognitively demanding tasks and impairs 73 endurance  performance.  The  neuropsychophysiological  mechanisms  underlying  this  decreased 74 performance remain unclear, with suggestion that mental fatigue may disrupt motor command and 75 consequently muscle activation. We aimed to test this hypothesis in a repeated cross-over design study 76 in which 18 participants completed two experimental sessions involving a time-to-task failure cycling test 77 at 80% of peak power output. Each cycling task was preceded by 1h of a prolonged Stroop task (Stroop 78 condition) or a neutral control task (Control condition). Mental fatigue was assessed using a visual analog 79 scale anchored with "not fatigued at all" and "extremely fatigued". Perception of effort and surface 80 electromyography from ten lower-limb muscles of the right leg were recorded at regular intervals during 81 cycling. Mental fatigue was higher in the Stroop compared to the Control condition ( p = .002). Endurance 82 cycling time was shorter in the Stroop than in the Control condition (887 ± 284 s vs. 998 ± 379 s, 83 respectively; −111 ± 160 s,  p = .009). No significant differences in electromyography parameters were 84 observed between Stroop and Control conditions, for any muscle ( p > .05). Perception of effort was 85 higher in the Stroop condition from the onset of the cycling task ( p = .006), and the rate of increase in 86 perception of effort was significantly higher in the Stroop than Control condition ( p = .031). Our findings 87 do not support the hypothesis that mental fatigue alters motor control or increases central motor 88 command, as no changes in muscle activation were detected. Conversely, our results reinforce the 89 notion that prolonged cognitive engagement impairs endurance performance primarily through an 90 increased perception of effort. Future research should consider combining surface electromyography 91 with more sensitive neurophysiological techniques to investigate potential subtle changes in motor drive 92 during dynamic, whole-body tasks under mental fatigue. 93
