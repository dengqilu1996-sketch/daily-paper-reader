## 摘要
持续数周的抗阻训练主要通过增强从神经系统到肌肉的驱动力的神经适应来增加最大力量。尽管这些适应在次最大力量收缩期间的运动单位水平上已有充分记录，但最大随意收缩期间力量增加的机制仍知之甚少。这是由于在最大力量任务中进行纵向运动单位追踪的经典技术限制。在此，我们解决了这一技术挑战，从而能够在单侧抗阻训练后研究受训练和未受训练肢体的最大随意收缩期间的运动单位适应。在为期4周的单侧抗阻训练干预前后，从双臂的肱二头肌记录高密度表面肌电图，并通过串联三个约5秒的最大随意收缩试验，在最大随意收缩期间跨阶段纵向追踪相同的运动单位。单侧训练增加了受训练肢体的最大力量（+16%），并将力量转移到了未受训练肢体（+8%）。在双臂中，训练后的最大收缩表现出更大的肌电振幅、更快的肌纤维传导速度和更高的运动单位放电率，表明对运动神经元池的神经驱动增强。这些适应与最大力量的改善密切相关（所有R² > 0.7）。重要的是，纵向运动单位追踪揭示了运动单位池内非均匀的适应：基线传导速度较高的运动单位（表明募集阈值较高）表现出最大的放电率训练前后增加，而较低阈值的单位变化较小。总的来说，这些发现表明，最大力量的增加及其向未受训练肢体的转移主要是由最大随意收缩期间更高阈值运动单位的频率编码增强所介导的。

## Abstract
Resistance training lasting a few weeks increases maximal force mainly through neural adaptations that enhance the drive from the nervous system to muscle. While these adaptations have been well documented at the motor unit (MU) level during submaximal force contractions, the mechanisms underlying force increases during maximal voluntary contractions are poorly understood. This is due to a classic technical limitation in tracking MUs longitudinally during maximal force tasks. Here, we solved this technical challenge, enabling the investigation of MU adaptations during MVCs in both the trained and untrained limbs following unilateral resistance training. High-density surface electromyography was recorded from the biceps brachii of both limbs before and after a 4-week unilateral resistance-training intervention, and the same MUs were longitudinally tracked across sessions during MVCs by concatenation of three MVC trials of ~5-s each.Unilateral training increased maximal force in the trained limb (+16%) and induced strength transfer to the untrained limb (+8%). In both limbs, maximal contractions after training were characterized by greater EMG amplitude, faster muscle-fiber conduction velocity, and higher MU discharge rates, indicating enhanced neural drive to the motoneuron pool. These adaptations were strongly associated with improvements in maximal force (R2 > 0.7 for all). Importantly, longitudinal MU tracking revealed a non-uniform adaptation across the MU pool: MUs with higher baseline conduction velocity, indicative of higher recruitment threshold, exhibited the largest pre-post increases in discharge rate, whereas lower-threshold units showed smaller changes. Collectively, these findings demonstrate that gains in maximal force and their transfer to the untrained limb are primarily mediated by enhanced rate coding of higher-threshold MUs during MVCs.
